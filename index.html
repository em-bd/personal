Pumpkin Tracker


Every year, Reiman Gardens hosts a Spirit of the Gardens event in October, where 1200+ pumpkins are carved and displayed for each week of the event. Volunteers are requested to help cut out stencils, trace stencils onto pumpkins, and carve them. All pumpkin statuses are stored in a single excel sheet, where Admins must manually adjust the statuses of each stencil—whether they've been printed, cut out, traced, carved, and put out for display. Additionally, volunteers for Spirits of the Gardens will help select stencils to be used for each week of the event, which takes an additional step of marking down which week(s) a stencil is available for. As the stencil database keeps growing, this process is becoming egregiously time-consuming.

To aid with this issue, I'm currently working with Nathan Brockman—one of the faculty at Reiman Gardens—on a team of Software Engineers to develop a app called Pumpkin Tracker to make tracking information quickly and accurately. Pumpkin Tracker plans to facilitate interactions with the stencil database by allowing Event Admins to easily add, delete, update, and archive stencils with the simple click of a few buttons as opposed to manually editing a row in an excel sheet that contains 1600+ entries. A Stencil Management page will also been included, which allows Admins to update stencil statuses by either confirming or overriding their current status. We implemented barcode scanner functionality to provide a batch update option, where Admins can quickly scan barcodes of any stencils that were returned and further expedite the process. A Volunteer app is also being developed, which both provides engagement for those volunteering to help cut and trace stencils as well as eventually provide time estimates for how much work each stencil will take and updates to stencil statuses that will then be confirmed by an Admin.

I am primarily working on Frontend for the development of the Mobile and Web Application, which is being set up as a React app. This involves working extensively with ReactDOM, JavaScript, and HTML/CSS. Our database is running on SQLite and is planned to be hosted on a DigitalOcean Droplet in August.
